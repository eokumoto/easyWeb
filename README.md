# EasyWeb

> Helping people navigate the internet with clarity and confidence.

EasyWeb is a browser designed to help people who feel less confident online understand websites, recognize potential scams, and ask someone they trust for help when needed.

It was inspired by watching members of my own family struggle with modern websites; not because they lacked intelligence, but because today's internet has become increasingly complicated.

---

## Features

### Ask EasyWeb

Ask questions about supported websites in plain language.

EasyWeb explains information in a simple, approachable way and can point directly to the relevant section using **"Show me on the page."**

---

### Scam Awareness

EasyWeb helps users recognize warning signs such as:

- lookalike phishing websites
- suspicious shopping pages
- urgency tactics
- unclear refund information
- risky payment requests

---

### Trusted Helper Companion

Users can optionally pair EasyWeb with a trusted family member or friend.

Helpers can:

- manage bookmarks
- respond to help requests
- provide reassurance when someone is unsure about a website

The browser user remains in control and chooses when to ask for help.

---

### Demo Websites

The prototype includes several realistic demo websites, including:

- HealthPlus
- VitaGlow
- rob1ox.com

These demonstrate how EasyWeb explains websites, highlights information, warns about suspicious pages, and connects users with a trusted helper.

# Using EasyWeb

The landing page provides access to two experiences:

- **EasyWeb Browser**
- **EasyWeb Helper**

For the full demo:

1. Open the Browser and Helper App in separate tabs.
2. Pair them using the pairing code.
3. Add demo websites from the Helper's Side.
4. Browse those websites in EasyWeb Browser.

# Inspiration

EasyWeb was inspired by my own family.

Watching older family members navigate modern websites made me realize that many people don't struggle because they lack the ability to use technology; they struggle because today's internet has become overwhelming.

EasyWeb was built to make browsing feel less intimidating and help people understand what's in front of them before they click.
